Proper nutrition is essential for young athletes to perform at their best and recover properly. Here's a guide to fueling your basketball training with the right foods.

## Carbohydrates for Energy

Carbohydrates are the primary fuel source for high-intensity exercise. Include whole grains, fruits, and vegetables in your diet to maintain energy levels throughout practices and games.

## Protein for Muscle Recovery

Protein helps repair and build muscle tissue. Good sources include lean meats, eggs, dairy, beans, and nuts. Aim to include protein in every meal and snack.

## Hydration is Key

Dehydration can significantly impact performance. Drink water before, during, and after activities. For longer sessions, consider electrolyte drinks to replace lost minerals.

## Pre-Game Nutrition

Eat a balanced meal 2-3 hours before games or practices. Focus on complex carbs and lean proteins. Avoid heavy, fatty foods that can slow you down.

## Post-Train Recovery

Within 30-60 minutes after training, consume a snack combining protein and carbs. This helps replenish energy stores and supports muscle recovery.

## Avoid Processed Foods

Limit sugary drinks, fast food, and processed snacks. These can cause energy crashes and don't provide the nutrients growing athletes need.
